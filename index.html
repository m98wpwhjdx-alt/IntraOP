Clinical Guardrails

Common bedside formulamEq = 0.3 × kg × base deficit
Alternative HCO₃⁻ formulamEq = 0.5 × kg × ΔHCO₃⁻
8.4% preparation1 mEq/mL, hypertonic
Practical approachGive ¼–½ dose, reassess
Use only when clinically indicated, usually for severe acidemia or specific indications. Correct hypovolaemia, ventilation, hypoxia, hyperkalaemia, sepsis/lactate source and renal causes concurrently.
Risks: hypernatraemia, hyperosmolality, volume load, CO₂ generation/paradoxical intracellular acidosis, hypokalaemia, reduced ionised calcium and overshoot alkalosis.
Calculator output is decision support only. Local institutional protocols and senior anaesthesiology/critical-care judgement supersede this calculation.

📐

Formulas Added

Metabolic acidosisExpected PaCO₂ = 1.5 × HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2
Metabolic alkalosisExpected PaCO₂ = 0.7 × HCO₃⁻ + 20 ± 5
Equivalent alkalosis formExpected PaCO₂ = 40 + 0.7 × (HCO₃⁻ − 24) ± 5
Interpret only in the clinical context and with pH/base excess. A measured PaCO₂ outside the expected range suggests an additional primary respiratory process or inadequate/ excessive ventilatory compensation.

Fasting deficit = maintenance × NPO hours. Third-space: minimal 1, moderate 4, moderate-high 6, major 8 mL/kg/hr. Sodium bicarbonate: mEq = 0.3 × kg × base deficit or 0.5 × kg × (target HCO₃⁻ − measured HCO₃⁻), commonly giving partial correction first. Respiratory compensation: Winter formula PaCO₂ = 1.5 × HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2 for metabolic acidosis; metabolic alkalosis expected PaCO₂ = 0.7 × HCO₃⁻ + 20 ± 5. QBL added from supplied KarthikMD Intraoperative QBL Reference: 1 g fluid weight = 1 mL blood; net suction blood = canister volume − irrigation introduced; total QBL = (canister − irrigation) + (soiled sponges − baseline weight); QBL interpretation should be body-weight/EBV adjusted and clinically correlated.
